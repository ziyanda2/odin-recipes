Glazed Meatloaf

Description

Glazed meatloaf is a comforting and flavorful dish that combines juicy, seasoned ground meat with a deliciously sweet and tangy glaze. The meatloaf is typically made with a blend of ground beef, breadcrumbs, eggs, and spices, shaped into a loaf and baked to perfection. What makes this dish stand out is its rich glaze—a mouthwatering combination of ketchup, brown sugar, and vinegar—that caramelizes beautifully as it bakes, adding a perfect balance of sweetness and acidity.

This hearty dish is a favorite for family dinners, offering a tender, moist texture with a slightly crisp exterior. Served alongside mashed potatoes, roasted vegetables, or a fresh salad, glazed meatloaf is a comforting meal that never goes out of style. Plus, leftovers make for delicious sandwiches the next day!

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup ketchup
  • 1/3cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice, divided
  • 1 teaspoon mustard powder
  • 2 pounds ground beef
  • 3 slices bread, broken up into small pieces
  • 1/4 cup chopped onion
  • 1 large egg, beaten
  • 1 teaspoon beef bouillon granules

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
  2. Combine ketchup, brown sugar, 1 tablespoon lemon juice, and mustard powder in a small bowl.
  3. Combine ground beef, bread, onion, egg, bouillon, and remaining lemon juice in a separate large bowl. Add in 1/3 of the ketchup mixture from the small bowl. Mix well and place in a 5x9-inch loaf pan.
  4. Bake in the preheated oven for 1 hour. Drain any excess fat, coat with remaining ketchup mixture, and bake for 10 more minutes.
